The 90-second vent test

One simple sequence. No performance.

  1. 1

    Stand or sit tall.

    Let the spine lengthen without forcing posture.

  2. 2

    Open the mouth like a vent.

    The mouth does not have to become the main airway. Let it unseal the top of the system while nasal breathing stays easy.

  3. 3

    Soften the valves.

    Relax the jaw, tongue, throat, belly, and pelvic floor. Do less than you think.

  4. 4

    Orient gently.

    Face natural light if available. Do not look at the sun directly. Close your eyes, or look toward a mountain, tree, wall, or another object in the sun’s direction.

  5. 5

    Notice pressure, not oxygen.

    Look for a shift: less sealed, more open, easier vertical flow, or no change at all.

The model

Think open pressure system, not “take a bigger breath.”

01 / Mouth as vent

Opening the mouth can change the felt geometry of the upper airway even if the nose keeps doing most of the breathing.

02 / Body as channel

The jaw, throat, diaphragm, belly, and pelvic floor can behave like one connected pressure system.

03 / Thin ocean of air

You are not breathing from empty space. You are inside a surrounding medium. The practice tests whether the body can feel that contact more directly.

Keep it safe and simple.

This is a somatic experiment, not medical treatment. Stop if you feel dizzy, panicky, numb, or unwell. If you often cannot get a satisfying breath, wake up gasping, or have ongoing breathing difficulty, talk with a qualified clinician.
